Example protocol

Surface passivation for condensate imaging

This example page shows how a full protocol entry can live on the site without a hero section. It emphasizes quick overview, readable steps, and a clean reference link at the end.

Use case

Prepare low-background surfaces for sensitive in vitro condensate imaging.

Format

Overview, materials, workflow, troubleshooting, and linked reference paper.

Overview

The goal is to reduce nonspecific adsorption while preserving imaging quality and experimental reproducibility. This page is intentionally concise and can later be expanded with reagent concentrations, timing, and comparison notes.

Materials

  • Cleaned imaging surface or chamber
  • Passivation reagent system of choice
  • Buffer appropriate for condensate assembly and imaging
  • Control sample for background evaluation

Workflow

  1. Prepare and clean the imaging surface under the same conditions used for final data collection.
  2. Apply passivation treatment and allow sufficient incubation for surface coverage.
  3. Wash gently with imaging buffer to remove unbound material without disturbing the coating.
  4. Test background adsorption with a simple control before moving to full condensate experiments.
  5. Record the exact surface preparation condition alongside each imaging dataset.

Troubleshooting notes

If background remains high, compare preparation timing, reagent freshness, and wash stringency before changing the biological sample. It is often useful to document the surface condition photographically so that subtle preparation differences become easier to trace.

Reference article

Yao RW, Rosen MK. Advanced surface passivation for high-sensitivity studies of biomolecular condensates.
